Calling Script

Gatekeeper Bypass (Specific Delivery)

"Hi [Name], I'm hoping you might be able to help me. I’m holding a Revenue Leak Report regarding some specific errors on the Google profile that are blocking leads right now.

I need to get this to the person who handles the P&L or Operations to make sure they see where the drop-off is. Is that the manager or would that be the owner?"

1) The Hook (The "Auditor" Frame)

Goal: Establish immediate value. You found a problem. You are the messenger.

  • "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] in Ocala. I’m calling because I was auditing your Google listing and I flagged three specific revenue leaks where [Company Name] is losing leads to competitors right now."
  • "I’m not selling ads or seo. I just want to show you exactly where the pipe is leaking so you can plug it. Do you have 30 seconds to hear what I found?"

2) Discovery (The Fork in the Road)

Goal: Force them to categorize themselves.

  • "Usually, when I see these leaks on a profile, it means one of two things is happening internally:
    • One: You’re getting so many leads you’re too busy to answer them all.
    • Two: Or, the system isn't set up to catch them when you’re closed.
  • Which one feels more like your reality today?"

3) The Offer (Low Friction)

Goal: Triage only. Low commitment.

  • "Okay, that makes sense. Look, I’ve already documented the leaks in a 3-Point Diagnostic Report."
  • "I don't want to pitch you anything. I just want 10 minutes to show you the math on how much this is costing you. If you want to fix it, great. If not, you keep the report."
  • "Are you open to a 10-minute briefing tomorrow morning?"

Objection Handling

“Not interested” “Fair. Before I let you go — are you not interested because you’re fully dialed in, or because you’ve been burned by stuff like this?”

If “dialed in”: “Perfect. Quick check: what’s your average response time to new leads?”

If they hesitate: “That’s exactly where revenue leaks hide. Let’s do a 15-minute triage and you can decide if it’s worth attention.”

“Send info” “I can — but if I send info without context it’ll look like every other vendor email. Give me 15 minutes and I’ll make it specific to your setup. What’s better — today at [time] or tomorrow at [time]?” “Too busy” “Makes sense — that’s usually a symptom of the leak. This is 15 minutes, designed to save you time by stopping wasted follow-up and missed leads. Do you want to protect revenue this week or keep it on the back burner? I’ve got [two time options].” “We already have marketing” “Good — we’re not replacing marketing. Most revenue leaks happen after marketing: speed-to-lead, missed calls, follow-up, pipeline, show rate. Quick question: when a lead comes in, are you responding in under 5 minutes consistently? If you’re airtight, we’ll shake hands and I’ll disappear.” “How much is it?” “The 15-minute diagnostic is just triage — no cost, no pitch. If there’s one clear bottleneck, the next step could be a 48-Hour Fix. If it’s bigger, the next step is our paid Revenue Leak Diagnostic — positioned as a blueprint, not a sales call. The right price depends on the leak and scope — that’s why we do triage first. Do you have 15 minutes today or tomorrow?” “Why would I pay for a diagnostic?” “Because guessing is expensive. The Revenue Leak Diagnostic isn’t a free pitch — it’s a structured session that identifies the #1 bottleneck, quantifies impact, and gives you a clear action plan. Most owners lose more in one week of missed follow-up than the cost of a proper diagnosis. The 15-minute triage simply confirms if that level of diagnosis is necessary.” “We’ve tried agencies” “Totally understand. Most agencies sell tactics before diagnosis. We start with leaks and constraints: response time, follow-up, pipeline, conversion — then choose the smallest fix that moves revenue fastest. Give me 15 minutes — we’ll confirm whether there’s a fixable bottleneck — and if not, I’ll tell you.” “Call me later” “Sure — when specifically is ‘later’?” (pause)

If vague: “No problem — I can do [two specific times]. Which one should I lock in for a 15-minute triage?”

If they resist: “All good — I’ll send a quick reminder. What’s the best email/SMS?”

Close Script (Calendar Control)

“Perfect — I’m sending a calendar invite for [day/time]. This is a 15-minute diagnostic, not a sales call. You need to be in a quiet spot — we’ll move fast. If we confirm a leak, we’ll schedule either a 48-Hour Fix or the paid Revenue Leak Diagnostic as the next step. What’s the best email and cell for the invite and reminders?”

Lock-in: “On a scale of 1–10, how likely are you to make that time?”
If < 8: “What makes it a 10? Let’s adjust now.”

Discovery & Routing

Fix Matching Cheat Sheet
What they tell you Match to Fix
If it's response time Speed-to-Lead Fix
If it's leads not converting Follow-Up Automation Fix
If it's booked but not showing No-Show Reduction Fix
If it's scattered + messy Minimum Viable Pipeline (CRM) Fix

Discovery Questions

Use 6-10 total. Keep it conversational and select the section that matches their bottleneck.

A) Speed-to-Lead (Lead Response Speed Fix)

  • "When a lead calls and it's missed - do they get an immediate text back right now?"
  • "Do you have a backup person if the first person doesn't answer?"
  • "How fast do web/GBP leads get a response during business hours?"
  • "What happens after hours - do they just... sit until morning?"

B) Follow-Up Automation Fix

  • "If someone doesn't book on day one, what happens next?"
  • "Do they have a follow-up sequence over the next 7-14 days - or is it manual?"
  • "How often do leads slip through because nobody follows up after a quote?"
  • "What stops follow-up right now do you stop when they reply/book?"

C) No-Show Reduction Fix

  • "Out of 10 booked appointments, how many show up?"
  • "Do they get confirmations and reminders automatically?"
  • "Do you have a clean reschedule flow - or do no-shows just disappear?"
  • "Do you send reminders at 24 hours and 2 hours?"

D) Minimum Viable Pipeline (CRM)

  • "Where are leads tracked right now - CRM, spreadsheet, inbox, sticky notes?"
  • "Do you have clear pipeline stages, or does everything live in one bucket?"
  • "Do follow-up tasks get created automatically, or is it dependent on memory?"
  • "Can you see 'new leads' vs 'overdue follow-ups' in one simple dashboard?"

E) Money / Urgency

  • "Roughly what's one closed deal worth to you?"
  • "If you fixed just one of these - response time, follow-up, no-shows, or tracking - which would impact revenue fastest?"
  • "Is the bigger issue volume... or leaking the leads you already get?"

15-Min Diagnostic Structure

Minute-by-Minute Agenda
  • 0:00–1:00 Frame: confirm time, triage only, not a pitch
  • 1:00–4:00 Current situation: lead sources + handling
  • 4:00–8:00 Identify leak: response, calls, follow-up, pipeline
  • 8:00–11:00 Quantify impact + urgency + decision path
  • 11:00–13:30 Route: tactical fix vs full blueprint
  • 13:30–15:00 Schedule next step + confirm attendance

Qualification Checklist

Qualified = 5/6 Yes with Decision Maker required.

  • Decision maker? ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Meaningful volume/scale? ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Clear leak exists? ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Urgency (30–60 days)? ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Willing to invest to fix? ☐ Yes ☐ No
  • Good attitude / coachable? ☐ Yes ☐ No

Verbatim Triage Script

“Hey [First Name] — thanks for taking this. Quick frame: this is a 15-minute triage diagnostic, not a sales call. My job is to find where revenue is leaking and route you to the correct next step.

First: where does most new business come from today? … About how many leads do you get monthly? … When a lead comes in, how fast do you respond? … Do you miss calls during business hours? … What happens after first contact — consistent follow-up, or manual? … Where do you lose the most: lead→appt, appt→show, or show→close? … If we could fix one bottleneck in 30 days, what would impact revenue fastest? … Are you the one who can approve changes?

Based on what you shared, the leak is most likely [leak]. The best next step is [48-Hour Fix / Paid Revenue Leak Diagnostic]. I can book that for [Option A] or [Option B]. Which works?”
Routing the Next Step (The "Live Navigation" Close)

The Setup: “We’ve confirmed the leak. It costs more to leave it broken than to fix it. We don't do invoices or chasing. We treat this like triage—we fix it now. Are you near a computer or mobile?”

The Navigation: “Great. Go to [YourWebsite.com/fix]. You’ll see the page for 'Pipeline Diagnostic & Blueprint'. Scroll to the blue button 'Secure Your Spot'.”

The Lock-In: “Go ahead and click that. It locks in the time for our Strategy Session and processes the $350 fee immediately. I’ll stay on the line while you punch that in to make sure the calendar syncs on my end.”

Objection (Can you send an invoice?): “To keep this at $350 without retainer fees, we’ve automated the admin side. The booking and payment happen together to trigger the work order. I'm on the page now—do you see the button?”

Not Qualified: “Based on what you shared, I don’t think we’re the right fit right now — I’d rather be direct than waste your time. If things change, let's revisit. Want me to send one relevant resource?”

Quality Control + Scorecards

Call scorecard (10 points) — Pass = 8/10

Must include #1, #6, #9 to pass.

  1. Opened with confident hook in <20 seconds
  2. Asked permission for two quick questions
  3. Stayed in control (no rambling)
  4. Identified at least one leak
  5. Quantified leak (volume/time/impact)
  6. Confirmed decision maker + urgency
  7. Used IMPACTA language (revenue leaks, response time, follow-up)
  8. Handled objection without arguing
  9. Calendar control close (two time options + confirmation)
  10. Set expectations + reduced no-show (importance / reminders)

Top 5 Mistakes (and fixes)

  • Pitching too early: ask questions first, earn the booking.
  • Losing control on “send info”: “Info without context looks like noise. 15 minutes first.”
  • Talking too much: 70/30 rule. Ask → pause.
  • No clear leak: always hit response time + follow-up + drop-off point.
  • Weak close: always give two times + book live.

Coaching loop: pick ONE skill → roleplay 10 reps → apply same day → re-score next day.

Metrics + Performance Targets

Daily + Weekly KPI Targets (Revenue Focused)

  • Daily: 100 dials / 25 conversations / 4–5 bookings / 1–2 Closed Sales
  • Weekly: 500 dials / 125 conversations / 20–25 bookings / 5–10 Closed Sales

Conversion Benchmarks (Revenue Reverse-Engineered)

  • Dials → Connects: 15–25%
  • Connects → Conversations: 50–70%
  • Conversations → Bookings: 25–40%
  • Bookings → Shows: 70–85%
  • Shows → Revenue (Close): 40–50%
“If you’re behind” playbook (fix your funnel leak)
Broken Metric Likely Cause Immediate Fix
Low connects bad list, wrong times, caller ID issues Call 8–10am + 4–6pm, improve data, double-tap, local presence
Low conversations weak hook, no permission, gatekeeper control Shorten hook, ask permission, stop explaining—ask questions
Low bookings no clear leak, weak close, debating objections Force leak clarity; always offer two times; don’t argue—reframe and close
Low show rate weak expectations, no reminders SMS immediately + morning-of reminder + “what we’ll accomplish” + reschedule policy

48-hour catch-up sprint: add one extra call block/day (+60 dials), hit all “send info” by phone+SMS, re-attack “call later” with two specific time options, manager audits 5 calls and gives one correction.

End-of-day checklist (10 minutes)

  • All activities logged
  • Tomorrow’s appointments confirmed via SMS
  • No-shows moved into reschedule sequence
  • Pipeline clean (no fake stages)
  • Send KPI report (dials/connects/convos/bookings/shows)
